Now that you are trained to ignore the announcements... I'll send a real course related announcement.

Last week I mentioned that anyone interested could sign up for "the game" where you can practice social engineering techniques on one another. Send an email such as the following to godfreyko@byui.edu

Subject: CYBER 201 Cybersecurity Fundamentals: Controlled Social Engineering Exercise Consent and Authorization

I, [Participant Name], voluntarily elect to participate in the controlled social engineering exercise conducted as part of CYBER 201 Cybersecurity Fundamentals (the “Exercise”).

By signing this agreement, I acknowledge and agree as follows:

1. Purpose

The sole purpose of the Exercise is educational: to help students recognize, resist, and responsibly discuss social engineering techniques in a controlled learning environment. The Exercise is intended to promote cybersecurity awareness, judgment, and defensive habits, and not to embarrass, harass, deceive for personal gain, or cause harm.

2. Limited Authorization

I authorize other enrolled students in CYBER 201, and the course instructor(s), to direct approved and course-supervised social engineering attempts toward me, subject to the limits in this agreement and any written course rules.

This authorization is strictly limited to the techniques, systems, accounts, times, and boundaries expressly permitted by the instructor and this agreement. No permission is granted beyond those limits.

3. Permitted Activities

Unless further restricted by the instructor, permitted activities may include:

  • Verbal or written persuasion attempts designed to test my awareness of common social engineering tactics;
  • Simulated phishing messages intended to test whether a participant recognizes suspicious links, tone, or requests.
  • Pretext conversations designed to test identity verification, skepticism, or judgment.
  • Benign misdirection or persuasion meant to evaluate whether a participant follows safe verification procedures.
  • Use of training-only links, forms, or landing pages that do not collect real credentials or sensitive data and do not install software.
  • Other activities expressly approved by the instructor in advance.

4. Prohibited Activities

The following are not authorized under any circumstance:

  • accessing, attempting to access, or bypassing security on any account, device, network, file, or system without separate express written authorization;
  • installing software, code, scripts, browser extensions, profiles, executables, or other files on any device;
  • malware, ransomware, spyware, keyloggers, remote access tools, payloads, or any destructive or persistent technical action;
  • collecting, storing, transmitting, or using real passwords, authentication codes, tokens, financial data, government IDs, health information, or other sensitive personal information;
  • impersonation of real institutions, employers, banks, healthcare providers, law enforcement, or emergency services in a way that could reasonably cause panic, reputational harm, or real-world consequences;
  • harassment, discrimination, threats, blackmail, stalking, intimidation, humiliation, or repeated unwanted contact;
  • any activity directed at third parties outside the class;
  • any activity that violates university policy, law, platform terms, or instructor instructions.

5. Approved Targets and Environment

This authorization applies only to:

  • me, as a participant in the Exercise; and
  • only those accounts, communications channels, or training environments expressly approved by the instructor.

This authorization does not extend to my employer systems, personal financial accounts, healthcare accounts, family members, friends, roommates, or any non-course-related person, account, device, or organization.

6. No Lasting Harm / Safety Requirement

All Exercise activities must be designed to avoid harm and to prevent lasting technical, financial, academic, reputational, emotional, or privacy-related impact. Any scenario must stop short of actual compromise, persistence, data exfiltration, service disruption, or unauthorized system modification. This agreement authorizes only controlled educational testing, not actual exploitation. This aligns with the general principle that good-faith security activity should be designed to avoid harm.

7. Reporting and Debrief

Any successful or attempted social engineering activity conducted under this agreement must be promptly disclosed to the instructor in the manner required by the course. Participants must cooperate in debriefing and remediation so the Exercise remains a learning activity.

8. Right to Revoke

I may withdraw from the Exercise at any time by notifying the instructor in writing. My withdrawal applies prospectively only and does not excuse prior misconduct or policy violations.

9. Assumption of Limited Risk

I understand that the Exercise may involve temporary confusion, embarrassment, or inconvenience associated with simulated phishing or deception in a classroom training context. I agree to participate voluntarily with knowledge of those limited risks, subject to the safeguards and restrictions in this agreement.

10. No Waiver of Institutional Rules or Law

This agreement does not waive, limit, or replace any university rule, academic standard, code of conduct, technology policy, or applicable law. Any conduct outside the scope of this agreement remains unauthorized.

11. Instructor Control

The instructor may modify, pause, or terminate the Exercise or any participant’s involvement at any time for safety, compliance, or educational reasons. The instructor’s written rules and directions control in the event of any conflict with this agreement.

I have read and understood this agreement. I am signing voluntarily.
